Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The module aims to introduce the fundamental principles of fluid mechanics and thermodynamics and to lay a solid foundation for subsequent work involving civil engineering hydraulics, water engineering, building services engineering, industrial and product design.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A.	Understand the fundamental properties of fluids, units and dimensions associated with fluid flows.



B.	Apply the principles of hydrostatics in problems related to submerged and floating bodies.



C.	Understand the fundamental hydrodynamic equations and apply them to simple flow measuring devices.



D.	Understand the concepts of a thermodynamic system and state.



E.	Understand the fundamental principles and corollaries of the thermodynamic laws.



F.	Understand the physical interpretation and quantities of thermodynamic potentials.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The material in this module is delivered through a combination of formal lectures, tutorials and supervised practical sessions.  General transferable skills on data analysis and results interpretation are developed through the laboratory report and numerical assignments.
Syllabus
Fluid Mechanics

1.	Foundations of Fluid Mechanics: Definition of a fluid, Nature of fluid motion, Fluid properties, Relevance of subject and applications.

2.	Units and dimensions: Dimensional consistency, Dimensional analysis, Model testing and scaling: geometric and dynamic similarity.

3.	Hydrostatics: Hydrostatic pressure variation, Manometry, Forces on submerged bodies, Stability of floating bodies

4.	Basic Kinematics in Fluid Flow: Streamlines, One-dimensional flow, Mass conservation equation.

5.	Energy and Momentum: Bernoulli's equation, Energy interpretation, Static, stagnation and dynamic pressure, Flow meters, Pitot- and static-pressure tubes, Momentum conservation equation, Euler's equation.

Thermodynamics

1.	Foundations of Thermodynamics: Thermodynamic Systems, State Variables and Functions, Processes and Change of State, Extensive and Intensive Properties, Temperature and Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics.

1.	First Law of Thermodynamics: Thermodynamics and Mechanics, Internal Energy, Enthalpy

2.	Second Law of Thermodynamics: Time reversal, Reversible and irreversible processes, Heat and entropy.

3.	Overview of applications of thermodynamics in Engineering: Phase transitions, Heat Engines, Heat transfer.
