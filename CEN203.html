Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
To provide awareness and understanding of the principles of solid mechanics applied to engineering structures. In particular, the behaviour and types of failure of simple elastic systems and structural members used throughout civil engineering.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Apply solid mechanics principles to statically determinate and indeterminate structural elements such as trusses, beams, columns, and frames.




B. Analyse a given structure to determine the support reactions, bending moments, shear forces, bending and shear stresses, deflections, rotations and strain energy.


C. Predict the structural behaviour and failure of engineering structural systems by applying different methods and techniques used to analyse the structure.


D. Carry out laboratory experimental work using test, measurement equipment and software analysis techniques to collect and analyse data.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The material in this module is delivered through a combination of formal lectures, tutorials and practical sessions.
Syllabus
Deflection of beams:


Statically determinate and indeterminate beams and their identification, Deflection of beam structures under various types of loadings, Use of Macaulay’s method of integration, Principle of superposition.






Moment distribution methods:




Development of slope deflection equations, Moment distribution concept and examples for continuous beams, frames, and for frames that sway.






Column under compressive loading (Buckling of Struts): Euler buckling load for a perfect pin-ended and other end conditions. Buckling about major and minor axis. Effective length and slenderness ratio, Effect of eccentric loading.






Shear stress analysis and unsymmetrical bending:




Shear stresses in symmetrical/unsymmetrical sections. Shear flow of a sections, Unsymmetrical bending of symmetric and unsymmetrical sections.




  

Energy theorems:




Elastic strain energy, Castigliano’s theorem to determine deflection/deformation and rotation under various types of loadings.






Lab class: Experimental laboratories for buckling of struts, shear centre, and unsymmetrical bending. Computer structural analysis for frames and verified by manual calculations.
