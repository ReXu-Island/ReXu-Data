Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
To provide students with theoretical knowledge and lab-based training in pharmaceutical analysis and analytical instrumentation involved.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Demonstrate a critical awareness and understanding of the latest development of analytical techniques and existing forms of drug specimen

B	Evaluate the principles of the methodologies and apply them to analyze various drug specimen

C	Critically evaluate the data obtained from the instruments and interpret them properly

D	Understand, consider and apply research ethics and health and safety regulations to prioritize, arrange and organize lab work
Method of Teaching and Learning
Course content will be delivered by standard lectures and laboratory practice.
Syllabus
The syllabus would typically cover:

1.	Extraction methods in pharmaceutical analysis

The project will involve choosing suitable solvents and methods in extracting active pharmaceutical ingredients from several tablets or pills, removing excipients, and their subsequent analysis.

2.	Spectroscopic techniques (use of UV/visible light or IR spectrophotometry, NMR)

The project involves the use of several types of spectroscopic methods to measure the concentration of several extracted active ingredients (or purified) that can either absorb ultraviolet/visible light or not.

3.	Separation techniques: HPLC, gas chromatography, and mass spectrometry

The project will involve separation of protein-based active ingredients by HPLC or small molecule drugs by gas chromatography, quantify the level of degradation impurities in the samples, and confirmation of the ingredient with a mass spectrometer.

4.	Bacterial endotoxin testing and bioburden testing

The project will test the levels of bacterial toxin and bacterial counts present in water samples, raw materials, normal protein samples, commercial available drug samples.
