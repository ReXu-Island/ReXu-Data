Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module introduces various stochastic models for different actuarial and financial problems. The aim of this module is to develop the necessary skills to construct asset liabilities models and to value financial derivatives. The students are required to understand the developments of quantitative models to solve practical problems. The module helps students write actuarial professional exam.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Demonstrate and explain the theories on the behavior of financial markets.

B. Illustrate the advantages and disadvantages of different measures of investment risk.

C. Describe and identify the stochastic interest rate of return models.

D. Compare, construct and utilize asset valuations models.

E. Explain, construct and make use of liability and valuations models.

F. Interpret and apply the properties of risk-neutral measure in valuing option.
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module is delivered through formal lectures and tutorial classes
Syllabus
•	Measures of risk

Define common measures of risk; Relation to utility functions; Apply risk measures to investment environment; Perform calculation using risk measures and explain how the distribution of returns and the thickness of tails will influence the assessment of risk; Risk mitigation; Two common risks of insurance companies.

•	Models for the term-structure of interest rates

Models for the term structure interest rates; Desirable characteristics; Vasicek, Cox-Ingersoll-Ross and Hull-White models. Pricing zero-coupon bonds and interest–rate derivatives for a general one-factor diffusion model for the risk-free rate of interest; Risk-neutral approach versus state-price deflator approach.

•	Portfolio theory 

Mean-variance portfolio theory; Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM); Single and multifactor models for investment returns.

•	Liability valuations 

Ruin theory: aggregate claim process and the cash-flow process;  Run-off triangles: development factor; chain ladder method; average cost per claim method; Bornhuetter-Ferguson method.

•	Theories of financial market behaviour

Three forms of the Efficient Market Hypothesis and evidence for or against each form; Rational choice theory; Utility function; utility theory; Economic properties of common utility functions; Calculations related to utility functions; Dominance; Associated insurance problems; Behavioural economics; Kahneman and Tversky’s prospect theory; Bernartzi and Thaler solution.

•	Stochastic interest rate of return models

Concept of a stochastic investment return model; Moments of accumulated amount of a single premium; Moments of accumulated amount of an annual premium; Distributions of for the accumulated amount of a single premium and for the present value of a sum under the log normal assumption; Applications.

•	Stochastic models for security prices

Continuous time log-normal model of security prices; Brownian motion; Ito integral, mean-reverting processes; Definition and basic properties. Ito’s formula: statement and application in simple problems. Stochastic differential equations for geometric Brownian motion and Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process: derivation of solutions.

•	Credit risk modelling

Credit event; Recovery rate; Different approaches for credit risk modelling; Metron model; Two-state model; Jarrow-Lando-Turnbull model; Stochastic transition intensity.

•	Option theory

Arbitrage, complete markets and factors influencing the option prices. Specific results: valuation of a forward contract; upper and lower bounds for European and American call and put options. Binomial trees; Risk-neutral pricing approach to the pricing of equity options; Difference between the real-world measure and the risk-neutral measure; Pricing via state-price deflators; Black–Scholes derivative-pricing model; Apply in simple models as binomial model and Black-Scholes model and demonstrate equivalence to risk-neutral pricing; Greeks.

•	Models for the term-structure of interest rates

Models for the term structure interest rates: desirable characteristics; Vasicek, Cox-Ingersoll-Ross and Hull-White models. Pricing zero-coupon bonds and interest–rate derivatives for a general one-factor diffusion model for the risk-free rate of interest: risk-neutral approach versus state-price deflator approach.
