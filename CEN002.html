Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The aim of this Year 1 module is to prepare students for study on the BEng Civil Engineering and BEng Architectural Engineering programmes. The module aims to introduce students to the historical context of the Civil and Architectural Engineering disciplines from antiquity to present, as well as to introduce the fundamentals of the engineering design process and communication methods.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A.Understand the process of ‘engineering design’ and its various stages of implementation.

B.Understand how the most common engineering sketching and drawing methods are used to represent concepts and objects.

C.Demonstrate familiarity with the basic terminology and sustainability and moral principles used in engineering practice.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The module is delivered through formal lectures and associated tutorials.



Students may be provided with language/study skills support from the English Language Centre (ELC) to help them engage with module content. Some sessions on this module may be delivered by ELC tutors, with the aim of assisting students with language/study skills requirements for the module.
Syllabus
1.	Civil and Architectural Engineering: Historical evolution and pioneers.

2.	Basic concepts and terminology.

3.	Structures and Buildings: Elements and design process.

4.	Engineering Drawings 1: Introduction to engineering drawings, sketching, essential views, orthographic projections.

5.	Engineering Drawings 2: Additional views and drawing information.

6.	Sustainability in Engineering and Ethics: Sustainability issues in civil and architectural engineering, introduction to moral principles used in engineering practice.
