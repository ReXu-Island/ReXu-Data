Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The aim of this module is to:



Introduce students to the different education systems in the Asia-Pacific region for a critical comparison for similarities and differences, at the macro, institutional and stakeholder level
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:

A.	Critically assess the different education systems within the Asian-Pacific region

B.	Identify strengths and weaknesses of global education policy and practices at a number of levels within the region

C.	Develop and sustain a detailed coherent analysis of the education systems within distinct parts of the Asian-Pacific region
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching sessions are interactive and divided into two components: lectures and seminars. The seminars will build on the material covered in the lectures, going into more details on the cases studied.
Syllabus
Using case study, each week/two weeks a new region or country within the Asian-Pacific region will be analyzed, including but not limited to Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) policy, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam, Japan, Philippines as well as China. 



An online discussion community, between other similar programmes delivered in ASEAN countries will be created to enhance the study experience.



The module will cover a number of topics, which may include:

•	Comparative analysis of the educational cultures in the Asian-Pacific region

•	Impact and contribution of ASEAN policy to global education

•	Specific characteristics of Asian-Pacific region’s education practices

•	Diversity of education within the region, and its value

•	Countries specific effective practices of education

•	Common values of education and its role in global education
