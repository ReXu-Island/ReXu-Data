Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module is designed to:

Enable students to record audio in a wide range of location environments 

Enable students to begin their understanding of the importance of sound in film production during production and post-production
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Operate at a basic and safe level when setting up and using location sound recording equipment.

B	Demonstrate a practical understanding of the relationship between the moving image and sound.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching sessions are divided into three components (Lectures, technical screenings and practical workshops).



Lectures will focus on the art of sound recording.



Technical screenings will allow technical discussion about the development of motion picture sound and Foley.



An intensive series of practical workshops will equip the students with the technical skills, understanding of location sound recording and practical troubleshooting of equipment use.



Within lab work the emphasis will be on the manipulation of audio using software techniques

practical troubleshooting of equipment use.
Syllabus
Typically, they syllabus for this module will cover:

The basic physical properties of sound

Sound and image: relationship and meaning

Microphones, the various types, their properties and differences

Connecting up and operational requirements of location and studio recording equipment

Synchronisation and post production

Understanding a script and a film’s sound requirement

Screenings
