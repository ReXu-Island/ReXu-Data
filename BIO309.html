Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The aim of this module is to provide students with a basic and in-depth understanding of core concepts in modern molecular immunology.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Define in detail the fundamental principles of modern immunology

B. Define the molecules, pathways and cells involved in innate and adaptive immune responses, and how immune responses are regulated and co-ordinated.

C. Explain and evaluate the current understanding of the mechanisms underlying some important immune disorders and inflammatory diseases
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching strategy is a series of formal lectures.
Syllabus
Topic 1: An introduction and overview to immune system 



History and milestone discoveries in immunology; Immune organs and functions: primary immune organs and secondary immune organs; Immune cells: myeloid cells and lymphoid cells; Phagocytes; Antigen presenting cells; Immune responses: innate immunity and adaptive immunity; Introduction of immune molecules: cytokines/chemokines/complement; Overview, perspectives of immune system.





Topic 2: Innate immunity and inflammation 





Definition, functions and major features of innate immunity; Cells of innate immunity and functions; NK cell recognition and killing mechanisms; Phagocytosis; Pathogen-recognition receptors (PRR); PAMPs (Pathogen-associated motif patterns); Toll-like receptors, ligands/signaling/NF-ÎºB signaling and activation; Inflammation: definition, inducers and major cytokines (pro-/anti-inflammatory cytokines); Diseases caused by innate immunity.





Topic 3 (Part I): Antigens, MHC and Antigen presentation 





Antigen definition; Major features: immunogenicity and immunoreactivity; Categories; Immune privilege; antigenic determinants/epitopes; T-cell/B-cell epitopes; Exotoxin/Toxoid/antitoxin; Tymus-dependent (TD) antigen and Tymus-independent (TI) antigen; Adjuvant/vaccines.





Topic 3 (Part II): Antigens, MHC and Antigen presentation 





MHC-Major histocompability complex; MHC I/II; expression on cells; structure; functions; antigen processing cells; Dendric cells: Subpopulation of DC, functions and major properties; Antigen capture and transport by DCs; Antigen presentation; MHC I/II-binding peptides: major features; regulation of MHC I/II expression; Endogenous Antigen and Exogenous Antigen; Two Processing and Presentation pathways: MHC-I endogenous antigens and MHC-II exogenous antigens processing pathway





Topic 4: T-cell biology and cellular immunity 





Thymocyte development, thymic selections; T-cell receptor (TCR) the other major T-cell co-receptors and ligands CD28/CTLA-4/PD-1/CD40L; T-cell activation; TCR signaling; Stages in the development of effector T cell (responses); memory T-cells; Role of T cells in host defense; Subsets of T cells: Th1/2, CTL/ Treg/Th17/Tfh signature cytokines/effector functions; The balance of Th1 and Th2 responses; Role of Helper T Cells in CTLs activation; Steps in CTL-mediated lysis of target cells; CTL killing mechanism; T Cell Exhaustion.





Topic 5: B-cell biology and humoral immunity 





B-cell development, selection and tolerance; B-cell receptor (BCR) and other major B-cell surface markers; Memory B-cells; Antigen recognition and B cell activation; Humoral (Antibody-mediated) Immunity; Th-dependent/independent activation of B-cells: Th-B cells interaction; Steps in production of antibodies by B cells; Antigen presentation on B-cell to Th cells; B cell selection in germinal center-Somatic mutation of V genes; Ig Heavy chain class (isotype) switching; Stages and Features of Primary and secondary B cell responses;





Topic 6: Immunoglobulins: structure and functions 





Basic Immunoglobulin structure: Light/heavy chains/essential regions; Fab fragment and Fc fragment: structure and functions; Antigen binding activity and Fc-mediated Biological activity; Five major classes IgG: Key features and functions; Functions of antibodies.







Topic 7: Complement system 





Three complement activation pathways; Complete details of each pathway: Classical Pathway/Alternative Pathway /Lectin Pathway, and the connections with innate and acquired immunity; The functions, regulation and the major consequences of complement activation





Topic 8: Immune disorders 





Hypersensitivities: type I-IV: 

features/process/mechanisms/pathogenesis/examples; Autoimmune Diseases: examples of Autoimmune Diseases/type I diabetes and mechanism; Immune tolerance; Mechanisms for Central tolerance and Peripheral tolerance to self antigen; Mechanisms of T- cell anergy; Immunodeficiency;
