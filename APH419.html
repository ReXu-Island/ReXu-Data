Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
Clinical trials are a type of research that studies new tests and treatment and evaluates their effects on human health outcomes. Randomized controlled trials are considered as the gold standard in clinical investigation for collecting the highest level of evidence in order to limit all sorts of bias. It is the most direct area where biostatistics applies in healthcare section. 



The aim of this module is to provide students with an opportunity to gain sufficient knowledge on design, development and analysis of clinical trials using appropriate statistical approaches.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Demonstrate a thorough understanding on principles of clinical trials and its approaches

B	Demonstrate appropriate knowledge on designs and classifications of clinical trials

C	Acknowledge standard operating procedures of statistical analysis for clinical trials 

D	Demonstrate familiarity with recent developments and issues connected to conducting clinical trials  and analysis
Method of Teaching and Learning
The module is delivered through a combination of lectures and tutorials over a period of 13 weeks. In lectures, students are introduced to the core principles, major methodology, and common topics and issues in the area of trial design and analysis. Tutorials are given as a platform to address any specific question or issue from individual students.
Syllabus
1. Fundamentals (week 1)

  1.1 Introduction to clinical trials

  1.2 Basic design considerations

  1.3 Basic statistical concepts

  1.4 Randomization and blinding 

2. Designs and classifications (weeks 2-3)

  2.1 Designs for clinical trials (parallel group, cluster randomized, crossover, titration, enrichment, group sequential, placebo-challenging, blinded reader)

  2.2 Phases of clinical trials (phases I, II and III)

  2.3 Classification of clinical trials (e.g. multicenter, superiority, active control and equivalence/non-inferiority, dose-response, combination, bridging studies and global, vaccine clinical, and QT studies)

3. Analysis of clinical data (weeks 4-5)

  3.1 Sample size determination (two samples, multiple samples, censored data, dose-response studies, crossover designs, equivalence and non-inferiority trials, multinational trials, comparing variabilities) 

  3.2 Analysis of continuous data

  3.3 Analysis of categorical data

  3.4 Censored data and interim analysis

4. Issues in efficacy evaluation (week 6)

  4.1 Baseline comparison

  4.2 Intention-to-Trent principle

  4.3 Adjustment for covariates 

  4.4 Data monitoring 

  4.5 Sample size re-estimation

  4.6 Multicenter trials 

  4.7 Multiplicity

5. Safety assessment (week 7)

  5.1 Extent of exposure

  5.2 Coding of adverse events

  5.3 Analysis of adverse events

6. Topic 1 - biomarkers and targeted clinical trials (week 8)

  6.1 Concepts and strategies 

  6.2 Biomarker development and validation

  6.3 Designs of targeted clinical trials

  6.4 Analyses of targeted clinical trials

7. Topic 2 – trials for evaluating accuracy of diagnostic devices (week 9)

  7.1 Study design

  7.2 Measures of diagnostic accuracy

  7.3 Reporting results 

  7.4 Sample size estimation

8. Topic 3 – Adaptive clinical trial designs (week 10)

  8.1 Concept of adaptive design

  8.2 Clinical, statistical and regulatory perspectives

  8.3 Protocol amendments and its impact

  8.4 Challenges and obstacles

9. Topic 4 – Traditional Chinese medicine (week 11)

  9.1 Unique features

  9.2 Basic considerations of TCM clinical trials

  9.3 Other issues in TCM research and development

10. Conduct of clinical trials – trial protocol (week 12)

  10.1 Structure and components of a protocol 

  10.2 Points to be considered and common pitfalls 

  10.3 Common departures for implementation

  10.4 Monitoring, audit and inspection

  10.5 Quality assessment of a clinical trial 

11. Conduct of clinical trials – data management (week 13)

  11.1 Regulatory requirements

  11.2 Case report forms

  11.3 Database development

  11.4 Data entry, query and correction

  11.5 Data validation and quality

  11.6 Database lock, archive and transfer
