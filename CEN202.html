Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The main aim of this module is to introduce the students to knowledge, techniques and tools required for management of civil engineering projects.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Evaluate and analyze time constraints in projects and produce optimized crash costs for projects

B. Apply Life Cycle Costing to civil engineering and construction projects with regard to Net Present Value (NPV) and Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

C. Assess health/safety issues and ethical issues in the construction industry and appreciate ethical decision making processes

D. Apply risk management process, tools and techniques to civil engineering and construction projects and appreciate different types of procurement systems in relation to risk management

E. Evaluate civil engineering and construction project with respect to quality control and management.

F. Demonstrate research skill and ability together with presentation of the research outcomes.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The module is taught through formal lectures and class tutorials.
Syllabus
Week:

 

1. Time management – Crash cost



2. Life Cycle Costing (LCC) – Time value of money



3. Life Cycle Costing (LCC) – Net Present Value (NPV)



4. Life Cycle Costing (LCC) – Internal Rate of Return (IRR)



5. Health and safety management



6. Ethical theories and ethical issues in the construction industry



7. Code of ethics and ethical decision making process



8. Risk management - Process, tools and techniques



9. Risk management – Program Evaluation and Review Technique (PERT) and Monte Carlo simulation



10. Procurement systems and risk management for civil engineering and construction projects



11. Quality management tools and techniques for civil engineering and construction project



12. Sustainable construction – Tools and techniques
