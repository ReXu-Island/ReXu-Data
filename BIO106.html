Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
Microbes are by far the majority of life-forms on this planet. This module is designed as an introduction to the diverse and unique activities of microorganisms. It will cover aspects of microbial physiology, biotechnology and ecology and form a basis for more detailed study in future years. The module will also provide training in some key practical skills. Many of these are not only relevant to microbiology but are also used in other types of lab-based activities.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Understand basic biochemistry and physiology of microbes

B. Acquire skills in key experimental techniques used in microbiology

C. Appreciate the roles that microbes play in nutrient and biomass recycling

D. Understand the importance of microbes to human life through relevant examples, e.g. food preservation and food-borne disease.

E. Apply, evaluate and interpret acquired knowledge and skills to solve problems in microbiology.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The course content will be delivered primarily via standard lectures, which will be accompanied by suitable lecture handouts (also available on the VLE), as well as via lab practicals. Students will also be guided to sections of specific textbooks and, if reading of specific reviews or source literature is required, then copies of these will be made available to students.
Syllabus
1). Overview – microbial diversity and the global impact of microbes; microbes in extreme environments



2). The bacterial growth - division cycle; handling microbes and measuring their growth



          

3). Biogeochemical cycles       

    

           

4). Methods in microbial ecology 





5). Microbial sensing, communication and responses 





6). Crop pathogens and symbioses.  





7). Biodegradation and bioremediation. 





8). Food microbiology: Food preservation and microbial growth.





9). Food microbiology: Foodborne diseases; Food poisoning.





10). Lab practical 1: Handling microbes 





11). Lab practical 2: Microbial ecology





12). Lab practical 3: Microbial physiology 1





13). Lab practical 4: Microbial physiology 2





14). Lab practical 5: Biotechnology
