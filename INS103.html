Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module provides an introduction to world history from the start of the 20th century to today, with a particular focus on the post-WWII international order. Social, political and economic developments in Asia, Africa, the Americas and Europe in this period will be examined within the larger framework of international structures and processes that affect all.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Understand  the major events and developments that have shaped contemporary international systems 


B. Understand the historical dynamics of globalisation and the formation of international systems


C. Develop awareness about debates about “history-writing”


D. Demonstrate analytical skills in the causal mechanisms that shape historical processes


E. Analyse connections and comparisons across different geographic regions in the understanding of world history
Method of Teaching and Learning
Lectures, seminars, oral presentation of ideas and private study
