Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module builds on the introductory skills, knowledge and understanding learnt in COM118, TV Studio Production I.. Working in groups, students will plan, script, produce and crew a studio production of their own devising employing the more sophisticated production skills they learn during their studio contact hours. This may take the form of an entertainment or magazine programme or a drama production.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	contribute to the development of a TV studio production programme concept;

B	understand and undertake a key role in TV studio production crew;

C	analyse, contextualise and apply theory in order to critique their production process in terms of concepts encountered in both TV Studio Production 1 and TV Studio Production 2.
Method of Teaching and Learning
Practical hands-on workshops,  group seminars and production tutorials, practical group project work, written analysis and reflection.
Syllabus
Syllabus will typically include:

•	Developing an idea; proposal, treatment and script for a TV studio production;

•	Production development and planning;

•	TV and Film studio set design and construction;

•	Health and safety in the studio and working with lights;

•	Lighting for studio production;

•	Directing;

•	Rehearsing and crew communications;

•	Continuity;

•	Shooting.
