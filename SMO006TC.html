Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module aims to provide an introduction to business in China.



In that this is an optional module, to well utilise the university resource, the module delivery is subject to meeting a minimum number of students (10).
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Demonstrate a basic understanding of the Chinese business environment from a Chinese perspective.

B. Show appreciation of the fundamentals of Chinese business thinking.

C. Show ability to comprehend the types of expertise required for the Chinese business environment.

D. Show ability to to conduct basic business, environmental and competitive analysis in the Chinese market context.

E. Apply basic skills in formulating business, management, and organizational strategies for China operations.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching philosophy of the module follows very much the philosophy of Syntegrative Education. This has meant that the teaching delivery pattern, which follows more intensive block teaching, allows more meaningful contribution from industry partners. This philosophy is carried through also in terms of assessment, with reduction on the use of exams and increase in coursework, especially problem-based assessments that are project focused. The delivery pattern provides space in the semester for students to concentrate on completing the assessments.



This module will be delivered by a combination of Lectures, seminars with case studies tutorials, as well as private study and preparation.



In that this is an optional module, to well utilise the university resource, the module delivery is subject to meeting a minimum number of students (10).
Syllabus
Topics will typically include:

1. Introduction to Chinese market specifics.

2. Chinese business values, ethics, and habits.

3. Assessing business opportunities in China

4. Market entry strategies for China.

5. Market research and understanding the Chinese consumer.

6. China’s competitive advantages and disadvantages.

7. Overseas Chinese business networks.
