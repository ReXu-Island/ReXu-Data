Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The module provides students with a critical understanding of the roles of place-making and urban design in urban planning and regeneration, and enables them to develop the the various urban design skills and techniques needed to conceive and present a place-making project.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Critically relate and interpret the key theories that support place-making and urban design practice



B. Present an advanced understanding and summarize the role of place-making in urban regeneration projects



C. Demonstrate an advanced ability to identify and assess place characteristics in different urban settings

 

D. Effectively combine urban design techniques to develop place making



E. Manage a comprehensive range of visual, oral, and written media and methods to explain design proposals effectively
Method of Teaching and Learning
Lectures and group discussions, supplemented by homework reading and a practical project
Syllabus
Theme 1: Defining Place Making and Urban Design

location, space and place; non-places; public and private space; social construction of place; urban design principles.

Theme 2: Shared focuses 

place and scale; place attachment and identities; assessment of place qualities; streets as places of urban life; urban design in existing urban fabric; neighbourhood identity and urban renewal.

Theme 3: Contested Realms

branding and iconic places; places and the tourist gaze; gated communities.
