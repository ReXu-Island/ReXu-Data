Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
for students on their programme.



The main aims of this module are:



a)	To examine and practice a range of specific academic oral communication skills in an English-medium environment 



b)	To develop students’ understanding of XJTLU’s Academic Integrity Policy and strategies to avoid plagiarism, collusion and the embellishment of data
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A.	Understand and use appropriate academic skills, language, style, and structure for specific academic communications

B.	Deliver a formal presentation on a topic related to a specific field of study

C.	Demonstrate an ability to adhere to academic integrity requirements
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module will be offered in Semester 2 of the Academic Year.



The teaching sessions are 1 2-hour seminar each week for 9 weeks, with additional self-study. 



As an hours-based module, students may miss no more than 4 class hours in order to be permitted to submit their summative assessment portfolio.



Throughout this module, tutors will use a variety of communicative language teaching/learning strategies to reach different types of learners.
Syllabus
Topics and skills will typically include:

• Advanced academic presentation skills for research-oriented communication

• Participating in academic discussions and seminars on topics related to students’ field of study 

• Supporting and justifying an opinion 

• Effectively providing constructive criticism  

• Self-assessment, peer-assessment, and critique
