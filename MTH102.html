Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
To introduce topics in Probability and Statistics and to describe and discuss the potential scope of their applications.

To present the basic concepts and results in Random Processes.

To train the students' ability to think logically and independently and to acquire the skills of problem solving.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. know how to describe statistical data;

B. use the Binomial, Geometric, Poisson, Exponential, Raleigh and Normal distributions;

C. have a good understanding of the basic concepts related to Gaussian random variables,

D. have a good understanding of the Law of large numbers and Central Limit Theorem;

E. have a good understanding of the basic concepts related to Poisson Process, correlation

function, stationarity and ergodicity.
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module will be delivered by a combination of formal lectures, problem classes, case studies and revision seminars.

2.1 Mathematical preliminaries

2.2 Gaussian random variables, Jointly Gaussian random variables,Representation of jointly

Gaussian random variables

2.3 Law of large numbers and Central Limit Theorem;

2.4 Poisson Process

2.5 Correlation function, stationarity and ergodicity in random processes
