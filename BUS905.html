Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module is compulsory for all IMBA students. The International MBA program targets Chinese and expatriate working managers at the middle and senior management level. Many will need specific support in the conventions of academic writing and speaking. LAN905, which is delivered in Semester 1 to all new IMBA students, aims to develop and fine tune academic writing and speaking skills so that the student, who may not have completed previous study in English, or who have been out of an academic environment for many years, are prepared to complete the varied assignments as required of IMBA study. 

The main aims of this module are:

a) To examine and practice a range of communication skills in an English-medium academic environment;

b) To deepen students’ understanding of academic English genres and stylistic conventions appropriate to their field of study 

c)To develop and practice presentation skills appropriate to their field of study 

d) To develop students’ understanding of XJTLU’s Academic Integrity Policy and strategies to avoid plagiarism, collusion and the embellishment of data
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A Produce writing which synthesizes and evaluates information and arguments from a number of sources 

B Understand and use appropriate academic language and style in written and verbal communications 

C Deliver a presentation using appropriate language, style, and visual aids.

D. Demonstrate competence in strategies to avoid plagiarism, collusion and the embellishment of data
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module will be offered in Semester 1 to the students joining the IMBA programme.

The teaching sessions are typically delivered over 4 weekends (usually Friday evening and all day Saturday.)

Throughout this module, tutors will use a variety of communicative language teaching/learning strategies to reach different types of learners. 

Students will receive guided self-study to reinforce the instruction. This may include completion of quizzes and tasks on ICE.
Syllabus
Skills will typically include: 

Critical reading and evaluation of source materials  

Note-taking from sources  

Paraphrasing, summarizing and synthesizing ideas from multiple sources  

Methods to create cohesion and coherence in written and spoken communication Structuring of academic texts and thematic/critical development of ideas in speaking writing 

Application of discipline-specific referencing conventions 

Academic style and formal register 

Developing an individualized academic voice in speaking and writing  

Presentation skills :Research, plan , design a professional presentation
