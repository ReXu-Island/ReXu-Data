Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
Students gain a thorough understanding of the quantitative applications of interest and related functions. Students are required to understand how to price various financial instruments for different interest.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Classify and calculate all kind of rates of interest, find the future value and present value of a cash flow, and write the equation of value given a set of cash flows and an interest rate.



B. Derive formulae for all kinds of annuities; classify the notation of annuities and formulas for the present value of financial derivatives

C. Calculate the outstanding balance at any point in time

D. Calculate a schedule of repayments under a loan and identify the interest and capital components in a given payment; Given the quantities, except one, in a sinking fund arrangement calculate the missing quantity;  Calculate the present value of payments from a fixed interest security, bounds for the present value of a redeemable fixed interest security;

E. Given the price, calculate the running yield and redemption yield from a fixed interest security

F. Calculate the present value or real yield from an index-linked bond



G. Calculate the price of, or yield from, a fixed interest security where the income tax and capital gains tax are implemented

H. Calculate yield rate, the dollar-weighted and time weighted rate of return, the duration and convexity of a set of cash flows
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module is delivered through formal lectures and tutorial classes
Syllabus
1. Time value of money 



Principal, interest, Accumulation/amount function; Simple interest/discount compound interest/discount, force of interest, the effective and nominal rates of interest, accumulated and discount factors, m-thly convertible rate of interest, equivalent interest rate; future value, present value/net present value, inflation and real rate of interest, the general cash flow.     



2. Annuities  



Present value, Current value, Accumulated value of annuity-immediate, annuity-due, annuity- immediate/due payable m-thly, continuous annuity and respective deferred annuities; increasing/decreasing annuities-immediate/due, increasing continuous annuities-immediate/due, and respective deferred annuities; and perpetuity.  



3. Loans and the equation of value  



Term of loan, outstanding balance, final payment (drop payment, balloon payment), amortization, sinking fund, the prospective and the retrospective methods; the equation of value for certain and uncertain payments and receipts, respectively. Repayment by regular installments of interest and principal.



4. Bonds; Fixed interest security and index-linked security 



Premium, redemption value, par value, term of bond; present value of payments from a fixed interest securities when the redemption is in one installment and the coupon rate is constant; upper and lower bounds for the present value of a fixed interest security that is redeemable on a single a date within a given range; calculate  price/running yield/redemption yield of a fixed interest security,  index-linked bonds under a rate of inflation; arbitrage; price a forward contract in an arbitrage free environment; the value of a forward rate at any time during the term of the contract; hedging implied by a forward contract . 



5. Cash flow models & Investment projects (Yield rate)



Par-yields; yield to maturity; apply equation of value to find yield rate, pricing of a bond, cash flow techniques for investment projects when considering the income tax or capital gain tax, the time-weighted/money-weighted rate of return, portfolio and investment year allocation methods. 



6. Term structure of interest rates and Duration, Convexity and Immunisation

Yield curve; Spot rate; Forward rate; duration and convexity in a redington, immunisation of a portfolio of liabilities; calculate the price or yield rate for a bond under the given term structures.
