Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The aim of this module is to extend students’ ability to use theory, tools and techniques of modern construction management and train the students with practical applications of project management and BIM.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A.	Produce a quantity take off and a cost estimating for a small construction projects. 

B.	Evaluate the performance of construction project by using earned value method.

C.	Analyze and apply the process of productivity improvement for construction projects.

D.	Apply of modern management tools to construction projects.

E.	Evaluate the importance of sustainable construction and its practical application to China.

F.	Utilize project management software and BIM software for construction projects.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The module is taught through formal lectures and class tutorials.
Syllabus
Week:

1.	Quantity take off and unit rate estimating for civil engineering and construction works

2.	Operational estimating vs. unit rate estimating and depreciation for civil engineering and construction works

3.	Tendering for civil engineering and construction works

4.	Earned Value Management

5.	Work study

6.	Workforce motivation

7.	Resource management

8.	Lean Construction  and Supply Chain Management 

9.	 Value Engineering/Management

10.	Sustainable construction –LEED, BREEAM, CEEQUAL

11.	Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

12.	 BIM
