Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The aim of this Additional Learning Activity (ALA) is to teach students about the different ways in which firms can raise funds, internally and externally, the role of capital markets in determining the terms on which funding will be available, the costs and risks of difference sources of financing, and the role of taxation. The ALA covers the capital structure of companies and their ability to raise finance through initial and seasoned equity capital raising, through a series of lectures and tutorials.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Demonstrate a deep knowledge of contemporary theories of corporate finance.

B	Understand the application of corporate finance theories and methodologies to different business contexts.

C	Analyse the financial structure of a company.

D	Evaluate different sources of financing.
Method of Teaching and Learning
Lectures, class discussion, reflective exercises, individual and team assignments.
Syllabus
1.	Different financing possibilities open to firms; 

2.	The role of the different stakeholders for the financing decisions of firms; 

3.	The capital structure decisions of firms; 

4.	Implications of tax for the financing decisions of firms; 

5.	Theoretical issues, ideas and arguments, and empirical evidence, that might influence the formation and outcome of financial policy initiation; 

6.	Mergers and Acquisitions and Corporate Restructuring
