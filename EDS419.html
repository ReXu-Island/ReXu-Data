Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module will introduce the basic ideas and skills in developing, implementing, and evaluating interventional programmes for children and families in educational and other informal learning settings. Different approaches to planning and evaluation will be introduced. Needs assessment, the principles of programme design, as well as the importance of programme theory will be considered. Specifically, criteria for assessing the efficacy, effectiveness and readiness for dissemination of evidence-based programmes will be introduced. It is a practice-oriented module and will also discuss values, assumptions and ethics in programme planning and evaluation.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Understand and reflect on the basic principles and theories in designing programme for family education deeply

B	Develop the professional skills to evaluate and analyze the family situation and the possible influences on children's development

C	Develop and practice the skills to design and supervise family education programme

D	Evaluate the quality of the family education programme
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching sessions are interactive and divided into two components: lectures and tutorials/workshops. The tutorials/workshops will build on the material covered in the lectures.
Syllabus
The module will cover a number of areas, including:

	Basic principles in designing programme for children 

	Programme theory

	Programme design (situation analysis, plan with logic)

	Programme material design

	Programme implementation

	Programme monitoring 

	Programme evaluation
