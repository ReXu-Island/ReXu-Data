Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This module aims to introduce a strong foundation to qualitatively analyse and understand a given structural analysis problem considering the behaviour of the structure as well as its engineering application.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A. Apply fundamental concepts of statical determinacy to geometrical stability analysis of trusses, beams, and frame structures.

B. Establish deflection shape, by bending moment diagram, and shear force diagram qualitatively without performing calculation and thereby understand structural behaviour.

C. Analyze and understand the behaviour of structures under various loadings, loading combinations and variations of member properties.

D. Predict the structural behaviour and failure of engineering structural systems beyond the elastic limit.
Method of Teaching and Learning
Lectures complemented by tutorial sessions 

to cover main theory, illustrations of qualitative analysis, examples, and tutorial questions.
Syllabus
Statical Classification:

Statical determinacy and stability, Geometrically unstable structures, Degree of freedoms in relation to indeterminacy of structures, Identification of zero and non-zero force members in trusses.



Beams/Frames:

Use of slope deflection method, Sketching of the deflected shape, Schematically sketching of the bending moment and shear force diagrams of beams and frames. The relationship between deflected shape and the bending moment diagram. 

Variations of member properties:

Behaviors of structures influenced by material properties and sectional dimensions, Change in bending moment, shear forces, and deflected shape of structures.



Plastic analysis of structures: Bending beyond yield point, Plastic moment of a section, Plastic hinges, and General theorems of determination of collapse loads. Examples of simple and continuous beams.
