Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
The module is designed to complement the theoretical knowledge developed in previous concrete design modules. It aims to consolidate students’ understanding of concrete structures design and provide them with a basis for designing concrete composite structures. The course also provides students with design and modelling techniques used for practical applications.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Review and complement analysis techniques of concrete structures.

B	Develop design schemes according to industry practices.

C	Apply theories relevant to composite slab and beam designs according to EC4.

D	Analyze and design steel-concrete composite slabs and beams.
Method of Teaching and Learning
The teaching is a mixture of lectures and labs sessions. The assessment is a coursework based on a composite design incorporating practical details. Formative assessment will be included during the course of the delivery period.
Syllabus
Design of composite structures according to Eurocode 4:

1.	Design of composite slabs

a.	Introduction: shear connectors, profiled sheeting and composite actions

b.	Analysis of internal forces and moments

c.	Design considerations and calculations

d.	Detailing and specification requirements

2.	Design of composite beams

a.	Static system, cross-section and actions

b.	Serviceability and ultimate limit states design calculations

c.	Detailing and specification requirements 

3.	Design of composite columns

a.	Serviceability and ultimate limit states design calculations

b.	Detailing and specification requirements
