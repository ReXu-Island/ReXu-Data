Description

Aims and Fit of Module(i.e. relationship to programme)
This course aims to provide the student with a foundational understanding of: (1) how effects of environmental pollution on human health are assessed, (2) environmentally responsible behavior research, and (3) how environmentally responsible behavioral interventions are evaluated.
Learning Outcomes(for accreditation and other reasons,sub-headings could be added to this section)
Students completing the module successfully should be able to:
A	Explain key concepts in environmental epidemiology 


B	Explain key concepts in the study of environmentally responsible behaviors


C	Critically assess environmental epidemiology research


D	Critically assess studies of environmentally responsible behaviors


E	Apply key concepts in social program evaluation
Method of Teaching and Learning
This module will be delivered through interactive lectures and seminars supplemented with real-world case studies. Students will also learn through independent research and peer-to-peer feedback.
Syllabus
Part I: Environmental pollution and human health


     1: Concepts in environmental epidemiology




     2: Causal diagrams – Lead poisoning case study




     3: Confounding – Ionizing radiation poisoning case study




     4: Selection bias – Methyl mercury poisoning case study




     5: Exposure misclassification – PM 2.5 air pollution case study


     

     6: Disease misclassification – pesticide/herbicide exposure case studies




     7: (Reading week)




Part II: Environmentally responsible behavior research




     8: Theories of environmentally responsible behavior – CO2 emissions case study




     9: Institutions for environmental governance – nitrate water pollution case study




     10: The environment in business decision making – resource extraction case study




     11: Environmentally significant individual behaviors – alternative fuel vehicle case study




Part III: Evaluation of environmentally responsible behavioral interventions 




     12: Concepts in social program evaluation (I) – human waste case study




     13: Concepts in social program evaluation (II) – recycling case study




     14: Oral presentation of intervention study proposal
